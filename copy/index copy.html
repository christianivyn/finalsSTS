STS Interactive Simulation

Algorithms increasingly shape what people see, think, and choose. This interactive simulation explores how algorithmic systems influence youth decision-making in everyday digital life.

Algorithmic curation increasingly shapes the digital environments that young people navigate, influencing what they see, how they interpret information, and ultimately how they make everyday decisions. Research by Swart (2021) shows that social media algorithms filter news and personalized content based on user interactions, yet young people’s awareness of these systems is often limited and context-dependent. This dynamic not only directs their exposure to information but also impacts their ability to understand and intervene in algorithmic processes, highlighting the importance of developing stronger algorithmic literacy in the digital age.

Final Reflection

This project explored how science and technology—particularly algorithmic systems and digital media—shape human behavior, decision-making, and understanding in contemporary society. Rather than relying on overt persuasion or direct argument, digital platforms influence users through subtle design mechanisms such as personalized recommendations, infinite scrolling, and attention-capturing interfaces. These systems persuade by appealing to psychological tendencies like immediate gratification and habit formation, raising important disputes about autonomy, awareness, and ethical responsibility in digital environments.

Throughout the analysis, a clear distinction was made between factual evidence and inference. Research-based findings demonstrated measurable outcomes such as increased screen time, reduced concentration, delayed consumption, and narrowed exposure to information, while inferences were used to explain how these patterns affect cognition, emotional well-being, and long-term understanding. Evaluating credible academic and health sources ensured that conclusions were grounded in relevant and verifiable data.

The project also highlighted the roles of individuals, organizations, and institutions in both contributing to and addressing these challenges. Technology companies design the systems that shape information flow, while media organizations influence public awareness and opinion. At the same time, educators, researchers, and mental health professionals provide strategies that empower individuals to maintain focus, resist distraction, and seek multiple perspectives. This demonstrates that solutions to societal problems require shared responsibility across multiple sectors.

Media plays a central role in shaping world events and public opinion, acting both as a source of information and as a mechanism of influence. Historically, media has always affected public perception, but modern algorithm-driven platforms intensify this influence through personalization and scale. These developments connect past media evolution to present concerns, explaining how historical trends have led to today’s challenges with attention, understanding, and polarization.

The issue intersects with geography, culture, economics, and politics, as digital platforms influence global communication, consumer behavior, cultural norms, and political discourse across regions. The societal impact is evident at local, national, and international levels, affecting education, mental health, civic engagement, and information accessibility.

To address these challenges, the project proposes solutions centered on algorithmic literacy, intentional media use, perspective-seeking, and self-regulation. Encouraging users to engage critically with content, limit passive consumption, and actively seek diverse viewpoints can reduce negative effects while preserving the benefits of technology. By integrating insights from multiple disciplines and media formats, these solutions offer a balanced and sustainable approach.

In conclusion, while digital technologies profoundly shape modern life, their influence can be understood, evaluated, and managed. Through critical awareness and informed action, individuals and societies can navigate digital environments more responsibly and develop a broader, more meaningful understanding of the world.
